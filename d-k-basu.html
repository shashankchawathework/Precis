Shri D. K. Basu v. State of West Bengal (1996)

Citation: AIR 1996 SC 1630

Bench: Ranganath Misra, K. Ramaswamy, S. Ratnavelu, S.R. Pandian, and others

Background:

The case addressed the procedural safeguards for arrest and detention under Article 22 of the Indian Constitution, particularly regarding custodial violence and illegal detention practices.

Issues:

Whether the procedural safeguards provided under Article 22 have been violated, and the extent to which custodial violence can be justified or condemned.

Judgement:

The Supreme Court laid down guidelines to prevent custodial violence, emphasizing the right to life and personal liberty, and mandated that police authorities follow strict procedures during arrest and detention, with proper record-keeping and independent inquiries.

Key Takeaways:

  • Confirmed that custodial violence violates Article 21 rights.
  • Placed the burden on authorities to prove lawful conduct during arrest.
  • Enforced guidelines for accountability and transparency in detention procedures.
  • Reaffirmed judicial oversight in cases of custodial abuse.

Significance:

This landmark ruling strengthened the protections against custodial violence, emphasizing accountability and transparency in police procedures, significantly impacting police reforms.
