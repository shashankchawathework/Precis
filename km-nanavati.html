K.M. Nanavati v. State of Maharashtra (1961)

Citation: 1962 AIR 605

Bench: S.K. Das, Raghubar Dayal

Background:

K.M. Nanavati, a Naval Officer, was put on trial for the murder of his wife’s paramour, Prem Bhagwandas Ahuja. The case centered on whether the murder was a result of grave and sudden provocation.

Issues:

Whether the accused’s claim of grave and sudden provocation justified the act, and if the jury’s verdict was reasonable on the evidence.

Judgement:

The High Court overturned the jury’s not guilty verdict, considering it unreasonable. The Supreme Court upheld the conviction under Section 302 IPC, setting aside the defense of grave and sudden provocation.

Key Takeaways:

  • The judge's power to refer cases where the jury verdict is unreasonable.
  • Clarified the interpretation of provocation in criminal law.
  • Highlighted the importance of correct jury direction to avoid misdirections.
  • Set precedent for reviewing jury verdicts in Indian criminal procedure.

Significance:

The case became a landmark in Indian legal history emphasizing judicial review of jury verdicts and established important principles regarding provocation and culpable homicide.
