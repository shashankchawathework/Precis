Navtej Singh Johar v. Union of India (2018)

Citation: AIR 2018 SC 4321

Bench: 5-Judge Bench (Unanimous)

Background:

This landmark case challenged Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code, which criminalized consensual homosexual acts between adults. The petitioners argued that it violated their fundamental rights to equality, non-discrimination, freedom of expression, and personal liberty.

Issues:

Whether Section 377 IPC, in criminalizing consensual same-sex relations between consenting adults, infringed constitutional guarantees under Articles 14, 15, 19, and 21.

Judgement:

The Supreme Court unanimously read down Section 377 IPC to exclude consensual sexual acts between adults in private, declaring such criminalization unconstitutional. The Court recognized sexual orientation as an intrinsic attribute of privacy and dignity, affirming the constitutional protection of LGBTQ+ rights and rejecting societal prejudices and discrimination.

Key Takeaways:

  • Decriminalized consensual same-sex relations among adults.
  • Affirmed sexual orientation as a fundamental aspect of privacy and dignity.
  • Emphasized constitutional morality over majoritarian social morality.
  • Advanced protection for marginalized LGBTQ+ communities under fundamental rights.

Significance:

The judgement is a historic milestone for gay rights and constitutional law in India. It transformed legal and social discourse on LGBTQ+ issues, providing constitutional recognition, dignity, and equality and shaping future anti-discrimination jurisprudence.
