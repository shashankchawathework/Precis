Justice K.S. Puttaswamy (Retd.) v. Union of India (2017)

Citation: AIR 2018 SC (Supp) 1841, (2018) 10 SCC 1

Bench: Chief Justice A.K. Sikri, A.M. Khanwilkar, D.Y. Chandrachud, Ashok Bhushan

Background:

A writ petition was filed challenging the constitutional validity of the Aadhaar scheme and the right of the government to collect biometric data, asserting that the project violated the fundamental right to privacy guaranteed under the Constitution of India.

Issues:

Whether the right to privacy is a fundamental right under the Indian Constitution and whether the Aadhaar program's collection and use of biometric data infringed upon this right.

Judgement:

The nine-judge bench unanimously declared the right to privacy as a fundamental right derived from Article 14, 19, and 21 of the Constitution. The Court upheld the constitutional validity of Aadhaar but imposed safeguards to protect privacy and prevent misuse of biometric data.

Key Takeaways:

  • Right to privacy is a fundamental right under the Indian Constitution.
  • Aadhaar program subject to constitutional and legal safeguards to protect individual privacy.
  • Emphasized the need for balance between state interests and individual rights.
  • Expanded constitutional jurisprudence on privacy and data protection.

Significance:

This judgment marks a significant evolution in Indian constitutional law, affirming the importance of privacy in the digital age and guiding the governance of biometric data collection to protect citizens' rights.
