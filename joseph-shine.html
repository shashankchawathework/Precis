Joseph Shine v. Union of India (2018)

Citation: AIR 2018 Supreme Court 4898, (2019) 3 SCC 39

Bench: Chief Justice Dipak Misra, A.M. Khanwilkar, D.Y. Chandrachud, Indu Malhotra

Background:

The petition challenged the constitutional validity of Section 497 IPC which criminalized adultery, arguing it violated fundamental rights, especially the right to equality guaranteed by the Constitution.

Issues:

Whether Section 497 IPC is violative of Articles 14, 15, and 21 of the Constitution as it discriminates based on gender and infringes on personal liberty.

Judgement:

The Supreme Court declared Section 497 unconstitutional, ruling that it was arbitrary, gender discriminatory, and violative of the fundamental rights to equality and personal liberty. The court struck down adultery as a criminal offense while recognizing it as a valid ground for civil divorce.

Key Takeaways:

  • Section 497 IPC is unconstitutional due to gender discrimination.
  • Adultery is decriminalized but valid as a ground for divorce.
  • Emphasized personal liberty, dignity, and equality as constitutional values.
  • Reinforced the separation of criminal law from matrimonial disputes.

Significance:

This verdict is a landmark in expanding gender equality and personal freedom in India, reforming laws around marriage while protecting individual rights.
