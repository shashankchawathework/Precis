Shayara Bano v. Union of India (2017)

Citation: AIR 2017 SC 4609

Bench: 5-Judge Bench

Background:

Shayara Bano challenged the practice of instant triple talaq (talaq-e-biddat) as unconstitutional, arguing it violated Muslim women's fundamental rights.

Issues:

Whether the practice of instant triple talaq violates Articles 14, 15, and 21 of the Indian Constitution.

Judgement:

The Supreme Court held instant triple talaq unconstitutional, declaring it void and illegal. The Court reasoned it violated fundamental rights to equality and personal freedom. The judgment instituted protection for Muslim women from arbitrary divorce.

Key Takeaways:

  • Instant triple talaq is unconstitutional and void.
  • It infringes upon the fundamental rights to equality and personal liberty.
  • Empowered the Muslim Women's Rights to seek legal remedy.
  • Called for a uniform civil code and reform in personal laws.

Significance:

This landmark decision redefined the rights of Muslim women in India, balancing religious freedom with constitutional safeguards, and inspiring reforms in personal laws promoting gender justice.
