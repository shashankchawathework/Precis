Indra Sawhney v. Union of India (1992)

Citation: AIR 1993 SC 477; 1992 Supp (2) SCC 217[file:57]

Bench: 9-Judge Constitution Bench (Jeevan Reddy, J. for majority)[file:57]

Background:

Challenges to the Mandal Commission recommendations and the Union Government's 1990 Office Memorandum providing 27% reservation for Other Backward Classes (OBCs) in central government services, implementing Article 16(4) for the first time at the Centre.[file:57]

The case raised fundamental questions about the scope of reservation, identification of backward classes, the 50% ceiling, and whether economic criteria alone could justify affirmative action under Articles 15(4) and 16(4).[file:57]

Issues:

Constitutional validity of OBC reservations in public employment, criteria for identifying "backward classes", permissible quantum of reservation, exclusion of the "creamy layer", and whether Article 16(4) permits reservations in promotions or only in initial appointments.[file:57]

Judgement:

A 6:3 majority upheld 27% OBC reservation as constitutionally valid under Article 16(4), subject to the "creamy layer" exclusion (affluent members of backward classes cannot claim reservation) and adherence to the 50% ceiling on total reservations (except in extraordinary circumstances).[file:57]

The Court held that "backward class of citizens" means socially and educationally backward classes (not purely economic), caste can be a relevant criterion for identification, but reservations in promotions are impermissible under Article 16(4), and carry-forward rules cannot breach the 50% limit.[file:57]

Key Takeaways:

  • Reservations under Article 16(4) are not an exception to Article 16(1) but an illustration of permissible classification; they apply only to substantive appointments, not promotions.[file:57]
  • "Backward classes" must be socially and educationally backward; caste is a relevant but not sole criterion; "creamy layer" exclusion is mandatory to ensure benefits reach the truly disadvantaged.[file:57]
  • Total reservations cannot exceed 50% except in extraordinary
